Calculating percentages in your head doesn't require a math degree. Whether you're trying to figure out a tip at a restaurant, find the savings on a store sale item, or estimate tax amounts, a few simple mental math tricks can help you calculate percentages in seconds.

The 10% Method (The Golden Rule)

The easiest way to calculate any percentage is to start by finding 10% of the number. Finding 10% is simple: just move the decimal point one place to the left.

Example: What is 10% of 250? Move the decimal point one place left: 25.0.

Once you know 10%, you can easily find other percentages:

  • To find 5%: Take half of your 10% value. (e.g., 5% of 250 is half of 25, which is 12.5).
  • To find 20%: Double your 10% value. (e.g., 20% of 250 is 25 × 2 = 50).
  • To find 15%: Add the 10% value and the 5% value together. (e.g., 15% of 250 is 25 + 12.5 = 37.5).

The 1% Method

For smaller percentages, find 1% first. Move the decimal point two places to the left.

Example: What is 1% of 800? Move the decimal point two places left: 8.0.

Now, if you need to find 3%, you just multiply that 1% value by 3 (8 × 3 = 24).

The Symmetric Trick (X% of Y = Y% of X)

This is a mind-blowing math shortcut that many people don't know: X% of Y is always equal to Y% of X.

If a calculation seems too hard, flip it! For example, trying to calculate 16% of 50 in your head is difficult. Instead, calculate 50% of 16. That's just half of 16, which is 8. Therefore, 16% of 50 is also 8.
