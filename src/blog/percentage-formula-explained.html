Percentages are used everywhere: test grades, financial interest rates, tax rates, statistics, and discount offers. While the word "percent" simply means "per 100", understanding the algebraic formula can make solving everyday math problems straightforward.

The Basic Percentage Formula

The basic formula to find a percentage is:

Percentage = (Part / Whole) × 100

This formula helps you understand what portion of a whole amount a specific part represents.

Step-by-Step Example:

Suppose a student scores 45 marks out of 60 on an exam. What is their percentage score?

  1. Identify the **Part**: 45
  2. Identify the **Whole**: 60
  3. Divide the Part by the Whole: 45 ÷ 60 = 0.75
  4. Multiply by 100 to get the percentage: 0.75 × 100 = 75%

Formula for Percentage Increase

When a value grows (like population, savings, or item prices), you calculate the percentage increase using this formula:

% Increase = ((New Value - Old Value) / Old Value) × 100

Example: A product's price goes up from $80 to $100. The increase is $20. Divide $20 by the original $80 to get 0.25. Multiply by 100: 25% increase.

Formula for Percentage Decrease

When a value drops (such as stock prices or discounts), the percentage decrease formula is:

% Decrease = ((Old Value - New Value) / Old Value) × 100

Example: If a laptop's price goes down from $1,000 to $800, the difference is $200. Divide $200 by the original $1,000 to get 0.20. Multiply by 100: 20% decrease.
