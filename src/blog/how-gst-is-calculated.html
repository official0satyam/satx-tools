Calculating GST is an essential skill for shopkeepers, freelancers, and accounting professionals in countries using a value-added tax system. There are two primary types of calculations: adding GST to a net amount (tax exclusive) and removing GST from a total amount (tax inclusive).

1. Adding GST (Tax Exclusive Calculation)

If you have a net price and want to find the final price after adding tax, use these simple formulas:

GST Amount = Net Price × (GST Rate / 100)
Total Price = Net Price + GST Amount

Example: A laptop costs $50,000 net, and the GST rate is 18%.

  1. GST Amount: 50,000 × (18 / 100) = 9,000
  2. Total Price: 50,000 + 9,000 = $59,000

2. Removing GST (Tax Inclusive Calculation)

If you have a total price that already includes tax and want to find the net price and the actual tax paid, you cannot just subtract the rate percentage. Instead, use these reverse formulas:

Net Price = Total Price / (1 + (GST Rate / 100))
GST Amount = Total Price - Net Price

Example: A mobile phone costs $11,800 including 18% GST.

  1. Net Price: 11,800 ÷ (1 + 0.18) = 11,800 ÷ 1.18 = $10,000
  2. GST Amount: 11,800 - 10,000 = $1,800

As you can see, the GST amount is exactly $1,800, which represents 18% of the net price ($10,000), not the total price ($11,800).
